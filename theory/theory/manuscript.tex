\begin{abstract}
Classical first-order predicate calculus utilizes a destructive negation operator where the conjunction of a proposition and its structural negation ($A \land \neg A$) collapses the truth valuation to absolute falsehood ($\bot$), triggering an unconstrained logical explosion via the principle of \textit{Ex Falso Quodlibet}. This paper introduces an Information-Preserving Deductive System (IPDS). By treating logical negation not as an identity annihilation, but as a topological spatial evacuation into an indexed vacancy configuration ($\varnothing_A$), we define a robust paraconsistent truth framework that preserves structural context at the metalogicallayer and arbitrary inference explosions.
\end{abstract}

\begin{equation}
\mathcal{T}(A \land \neg A) \equiv \varnothing_A

\end{axiom}

\section{Eliminationg the Principle of Explosion}
In standard deduction, the sequence is structurally destructive:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $A$ (Premise)
    \item $\neg A$ (Premise)
    \item $A \lor B$ (Disjunction Introduction on 1)
    \item $B$ (Disjunctive Syllogism via 2 and 3)
\end{enumerate}

Under IPDS predicate rules, Step 2 is blocked from universal substitution because line 1 and line 2 collapse into an isolated vacancy state $\varnothing_A$ that lacks the active truth property required to evaluate the disjunctive syllogism step on line 4:
\begin{equation}
(\varnothing_A \land (A \lor B)) \nvdash B
\end{equation}
This holds because $\varnothing_A$ preserves the specific syntactic boundaries of proposition $A$, preventing it from interactiong with or validating an arbitrary, unrelated proposition $B$.
